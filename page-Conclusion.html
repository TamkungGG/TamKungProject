Conclusion

Main findings of the study

This study focuses on the media environment in Chanthaburi province and Thailand, and highlights the importance of media convergence in shaping the future of media in the region. The media landscape has undergone major changes due to the rise of digital media and the decline of traditional media, which poses challenges to traditional media platforms such as print, radio and television. However, Thai media are adapting and adopting new strategies to survive and change their business models. The rise of digital media in Chanthaburi Province is driven by higher internet penetration and smartphone usage, and the media landscape in the region has huge potential for growth and innovation. Media convergence enables traditional media to adapt to the changing media landscape and remain relevant in the digital age, while also opening up new opportunities for content creators to deliver more media content to audiences.

In summary, due to the rise of digital media and the decline of traditional media, the status quo of media convergence in Chanthaburi Province is undergoing major changes. Radio broadcasting, in particular, faces challenges and opportunities in a changing media environment. An in-depth interview with the owner of "VD PROMOTION", a radio station broadcaster in Chanthaburi province, highlights several key points about the current state and future of broadcasting in the region.

The interview highlighted the challenges facing the broadcast industry, the strategies radio stations are adopting to stay relevant and competitive, and the importance of innovation and adaptation in the industry.. The future of broadcasting in Chanthaburi Province will depend on the ability of radio stations to innovate and meet the changing needs and expectations of their audiences.

Discussion on the Significance of Media Convergence Research in Chanthaburi Province

Media convergence in Chanthaburi Province, Thailand is impacting the way media is consumed and produced, resulting in new forms of media being accessed through multiple platforms and devices. The province's media integration process consists of four stages and is influenced by various factors such as perceived usefulness and social influence. The digital age has brought increased access to information, improved communication efficiency, and expanded market opportunities, but also challenges such as lack of infrastructure and competition from other media platforms. The province's broadcaster "VD PROMOTION" is adapting to the changing environment by leveraging digital technology, using social media platforms and investing in digital marketing techniques. However, the company still faces challenges such as maintaining high quality standards and adapting to rapid technological changes. Key issues facing “VD PROMOTION” and other media companies in the province include challenges in media convergence, monetization of digital products, transformation of traditional media, and policies related to media consolidation.

Recommendations for improving media integration in Chanthaburi Province can also be applied to other media such as television and newspapers. For TV, the key is to develop a clear value proposition for its digital offerings and to leverage existing assets and capabilities to create new digital services. This may include offering exclusive content, targeting specific audiences, or providing viewers with a personalized experience.

To increase engagement with viewers, stations can offer interactive and personalized content and services, such as incorporating viewer feedback and suggestions into their programming, offering customized playlists and recommendations, or hosting live events and competitions. To support the development of the media industry, TV stations in Chanthaburi province can collaborate with other stakeholders such as advertisers and sponsors, other media channels and platforms, industry organizations and government agencies.

Likewise, for newspapers, the key to success in the digital age is developing a clear value proposition for their digital products and leveraging existing assets and capabilities to create new digital services. This may include offering exclusive or original content, targeting specific audiences, or providing readers with a personalized experience. To increase audience engagement, newspapers can offer interactive and personalized content and services, such as incorporating reader feedback and suggestions into their content, providing customized news feeds and recommendations, or hosting live events and competitions. To support the development of the media industry, newspapers in Chanthaburi province can collaborate with other stakeholders such as advertisers and sponsors, other media channels and platforms, industry organizations and government agencies. Additionally, like other media businesses, newspapers must find new revenue streams, maintain their distinctive brand identity, and balance the need to innovate and generate revenue.

Suggestions for Improving Media Integration in Chanthaburi Province

With the decline of traditional media and the rise of digital media, the media landscape in Chanthaburi Province is undergoing major changes. This presents challenges and opportunities for media companies operating in the province, including changes in consumer behavior, increased competition and the need to adapt to new technologies and platforms. The adaptation process is driven by technological advances, changes in consumer behavior, and increased competition in the media industry. Radio stations, in particular, face challenges in the digital transformation of their services and must find ways to overcome them and monetize their digital services. To remain competitive, media companies in Chanthaburi province need to develop a clear value proposition for their digital offerings, leverage existing assets and capabilities, engage with audiences through interactive and personalized content and services, and collaborate with other stakeholders.

Additionally, media companies must find new revenue streams, maintain their distinctive voices and brand identities, and balance the need to innovate and generate revenue. Policies and regulations set by the National Broadcasting and Telecommunications Commission (NBTC) play a key role in shaping the development of the province's media industry. In order to promote the development of the media industry, NBTC should review and revise policies related to royalty payment and market competition to create a favorable environment for the development of media companies in the digital media era.
